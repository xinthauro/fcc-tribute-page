Nikola Tesla

Was an inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who is best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current AC electricity supply system.

  • 1856 - Nikola Tesla is born.
  • 1875 - Enrolls at Austrian Polytechnic.
  • 1882 - Begins working for Continental Edison.
  • 1882 - Identifies magnetic induction field principle.
  • 1884 - Arrives in the United States.
  • 1885 - Quits working for Edison.
  • 1886 - Forms Tesla Electric Light and Manufacturing Company.
  • 1887 - War of currents era begins.
  • 1887 - Begins experimenting with X-Rays.
  • 1888 - Delivers lecture: A New System of Alternating Current Motors and Transformers.
  • 1891 - Patents the Tesla Coil.
  • 1891 - Becomes a citizen of the United States.
  • 1893 - Nikola Tesla and George Westinghouse power the World's Columbian Exposition.
  • 1899 - Moves to research facility in Colorado Springs.
  • 1901 - Begins on Tesla's Wardenclyffe Tower.
  • 1913 - Patents Bladeless Turbine.
  • 1915 - Tesla and Edison ignored by Nobel Prize committee.
  • 1915 - Files lawsuit against Guglielmo Marconi.
  • 1934 - The New York Times publicizes Nikola Tesla's particle beam research.
  • 1943 - Nikola Tesla dies.
